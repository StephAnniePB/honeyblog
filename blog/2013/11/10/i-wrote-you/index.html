I Wrote You

Nov 10th, 2013 8:41 am

1979- Artifacts of familial love; I have pictures, troubling truths twisting into lies and an endless pain I can’t disguise.
1985 - Michael’s moon walking, Slick Rick and Doug E. Fresh are telling tales to children, and LL needs love.  I raise my hand, I’m five but Hip Hop knows I’m alive.
1988- Jazzy Jeff and the Fresh Prince tell me Parents Just Don’t understand my hand hits the page to say he ani’t lyin’ ‘cuz Mama may be tryin’ but she can’t hear me cryin’.
1988- Traded sweet Georgia Peaches for tart Wisconsin Cranberries; winced at their tang on my tongue wishing for those who speak natively.
1992- Black is beautiful you told me this as I fought the urge to let the knife kiss my wrists.
1994- I had to smuggle you in; left the Quiet Storm on nightly. Snuggling your lyrics I loved you holding me.
1995- Congress can’t constrict my music besides you gotta have parents present to be advised.
1996 - Joanna jumped my journal. Took it, thumbed pages, turned red at the words used to describe…situations.  Left me wondering if I’d ever have privacy; left me wishing All Eyes weren’t on me.
Anger hugs me from the inside out   I just spin track four on disk two “You Wonder Why They Call You Bitch…”
1998- Graduating going into my comfort zone but unfortunately I’m making those steps alone my soft-hearted gangsta’s now anotha one dead and gone.
2001- I quit life huddled inside despair licked the wounds of neglect refusing to allow my pen the pleasure of paper knowing Hip Hop and Poetry live there.
2002- Talib Kweli is telling me what it takes to get by Poetry yells in my sleep urging me to fight phantoms; put pen to paper.
2004- Reached backwards for KRS-One knowing and knowledge now available
2006- Nas says Hip Hop Is Dead tears tear the pages of my notebook why wasn’t I at the funeral?
2008- Hip Hop and I are voting for America’s First Black President!
2009- Mad City calls and I’m in the mood to risk it all; Melancholia moves in my bones. I’m in search of home.

2011- I left Hip Hop in search of Soul and found……me.
