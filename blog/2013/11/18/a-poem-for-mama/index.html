A Poem for Mama

Nov 18th, 2013 10:18 pm

You didn’t have to take me home
You could’ve left me there
You didn’t need to sit in court
Or listen to doctor reports
You didn’t have to care for me
Or help through the surgeries

You didn’t need to take me home
And raise me as your very own
You didn’t have to impart
Words of Wisdom in my heart
You didn’t have to believe
In whom I’ve grown to be

It warms me to my bones
You chose to pull me off the lurch
And baptize me in your church
You chose to give me life
Easing the pain and strife

Thanks for opening your door
And calming the storm at my shore
There’s something you should know
Everyday this feeling grows
It thrills me to no end
You’re mother as well as friend


Mother’s Day 2001
