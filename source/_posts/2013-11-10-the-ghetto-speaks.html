--- layout: post title: "The Ghetto Speaks " date: 2013-11-10T08:47:00-06:00 categories: - Poems ---
When people today speak of me
The Inner City
Their faces wrinkle with distaste
As they head for suburbia
Muttering about me being a waste
Oh how quickly one forgets
I used to be prime property
Golden real estate
Black eyes sparkled with pride
Happy to give directions
To their front gates
Though
Money wasn’t made
Hand over fist
They weren’t scrimping every
Nickel and Dime
In spite of the color line
Brimming with promise
My soul entwined their heartbeats
Then
r-e-v-o-l-u-t-i-o-n
Came soft
Soft baby steps at first
Pleading better education
And higher wages
My people gave their children
More reason to leave
Than to stay
As oppression mutated towards a new form
And
F-R-E-E-D-O-M
Became for one and all
You gave the silver keys
For your corner church
In for golden ones to City Hall
And all the things
You knew I lacked
Weren’t important ‘cuz
You didn’t look back
However;
Now when children
Carve out their existence
In these bleak streets
Don’t turn a deaf ear when they speak
Listen hard as they say
All the factories closed down
‘Round my way
Wake up suburbia
Where does that label say
Your clothes were made
Understand
A dangerous thing happened
When you changed your bottom line
“WE” became “ME”

And “Ours” became “Mine”
