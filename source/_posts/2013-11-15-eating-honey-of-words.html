--- layout: post title: "Eating the Honey of Words" date: 2013-11-15T08:31:00-06:00 categories: - Poems ---
“Poetry it isn’t you it’s me…”
My heart pounds, my parched throat refuses to release more syllables of the response he is waiting impatiently to hear
Poetry stands before me
His eyes very much like mine; his beautiful black body filled with tension poised corded muscle ready to strike
And for the first time I know of the truth of ire
We are standing at the crossroads
Where many a mere mortal has met with the devil
To discuss my decision to no longer put pen to paper
As if I am free to walk away from him at any time

This struggle between him and I has been continuous
And each time I leave my spot at his table
He watches waiting impatiently
As I trouble trouble to evade eating the honey of words

Those of you who’ve never been under the influence of the written word may not understand
But please believe
Eating their honey comes with a price

Sitting at his table makes Him Master
Requires me to share every need or want
Requires that I reveal every thought
Even the ones I think I thought

Sitting at His table makes my morals non-negotiable
Demands that I use my personal pain
To continue what Langston, Zora, Richard, Nikki, and Pac started
And for the first time I feel the responsibility of a prophet

“Poetry, I can’t handle this…”
How can I begin to pick up my pen
When everyday I wish to die
How can I accept my seat at your table
Knowing I have lost my ability to dream
Knowing I let Lupus take my reasons to fly

Tell me poetry; what is the point of eating the honey of words
If I am eating them alone
Who will love someone who cannot bear children?
Who will want someone with both Lupus and CP?
Who will want someone without a home?

My God, Poetry, how can you even look at me?


Poetry's eyes are the night sky when a storm is near
Black pools of bottomless fury
His facial features twitch with the exertion of control
And His thunderous voice rolls low and deep

"Stephanie, Stephanie, Stephanie...
 Loneliness and depression have stolen
Sweet, silken syllables from your pen
Grief and pain have poisoned pleasured pastimes
Convinced you never to trust again

Babygirl, the honey of words is yours for the taking
Here at this table
 You can have whatever you like
I am your gift from God
He painstakingly planned you picking up your pen
Therefore,

Nothing you write with it is a sin
