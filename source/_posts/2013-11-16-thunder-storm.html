--- layout: post title: "Thunder Storm " date: 2013-11-16T19:40:00-06:00 categories: - Poems ---
“BO-OOOOM!!!! CR-RA-AACK!!! BO-OOOOOM!”
Mother Nature’s fury finds me on the balcony
Her thick velvet voice raspy and tired
“Man believes he’s the only one
Who roll with Liberty and Justice
Their tempers flare
Furiously fast sparking war
But Mother Nature is who I be
And I’m about to teach y’all
How a temper tantrum should be done.”

Restless energy vibrates through the trees
Leaves swish; swaying frantically
Vegetation opens; animals take heed
Coal eyes glimmering
As tingles trapeze her spine   

Throwing up her hands
She stirs the heavens
Antagonized anger at the edge of free
I watch her emotions galvanize
Thunder rolls
Thick purple clouds explode
“This is for my man upstairs
Who shaped this sphere called Earth
Separated Day from Night
Ensured y’all would thrive
Only asking you to follow His Light”

Tension fills her brown body
Emotions seeking release; surge
Flashing brightly lighting the sky
She holds fire in its fiercest form

“This is for the animals robbed
Of their place in the Circle
For the ones imprisoned in zoos
Deprived of natural habitats.”
Lightning flashes; Thunder rolls
The rain continues
Warmer as her anger grows

“This is for the very vegetation that gives you life
The forest of trees
Which inhales poisonous monoxide
So you can breathe
This is for every natural resource you exploited
Tell me;
Were your worries
Worth raping me of my treasure?”

“Men of all societies are taught
To treat women as ladies
I AM MOTHER;
Ani’t I A WOMAN?”

Thunder Claps; Lightning flashes
2 a.m. soaked with her pain
Blinking as I watch the rain
Breathing deep I enter the exchange

“This is for the woman who grew me
Nine months
And didn’t reach out to me once
This is for the man
Who should have been Father
Poems cry because you don’t know your daughter.”

For a moment I swear her
Energy is focused on me
“I know you feel restless
Struggling under the weight
Of a life you didn’t choose
But unlike me
When you get the blues
You can pick up your pen
You have the freedom to begin again.”
