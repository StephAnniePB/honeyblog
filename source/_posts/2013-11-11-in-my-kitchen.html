--- layout: post title: "In My Kitchen " date: 2013-11-11T13:29:00-06:00 categories: - Poems ---
The revolution will not be televised
It will begin in my kitchen
At Sunday dinner surrounded by family and friends
As each plate of food hugs us from the inside out
We adults will travel back
To where the fabric of our lives began
We will share hard times and good times
Tales of mischief and talking with trouble
We  will share joy and respect
Knowing that coming together eases the struggle

The revolution will not be televised
It will begin in my kitchen
As my children sit enchanted at their parents inner child
Each one will have a memory woven into their tapestry
For the younger ones it will be the sights and sounds
And basking in the freedom to live out loud
For the older ones it will come
In the acceptance of more responsibility

The revolution will not be televised
It will begin in my kitchen
At this round table laden with the fruits of our labor
As we welcome the presence of the Lord and pray
Come Lord Jesus be our guest
And let thy gifts to us be blessed

The revolution will not be televised
It will begin in my kitchen
